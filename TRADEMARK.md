# AEGIS — Name and Logo Use Policy

**TL;DR** — The MIT license covers the code. It does **not** cover
the "AEGIS" name or the shield logo. This page is the policy for
when you can use those without asking, and when you need to.

> ⚠️ **Draft pending counsel review.** This document is a starting
> point, not a binding policy yet. Until counsel has signed off,
> assume the spirit of fair use applies but reach out before
> commercial use of the brand.

---

## What you can use without asking (free, no permission needed)

- **Plain factual mentions in technical writing.**
  "We use AEGIS to firewall our agent's tool calls."
  "AEGIS is a pre-execution policy engine for AI agents."
- **Documentation cross-references.**
  Linking to this repo, citing the README, embedding screenshots
  of the dashboard in your own docs/blog/conference talk.
- **Compatibility statements.**
  "Works with AEGIS." "Integrates with AEGIS." — provided the
  statement is accurate.
- **Academic / research citations.**
  Mentioning AEGIS in a paper, dataset description, or thesis is
  always fine.
- **Forks and contributions.**
  A fork on GitHub can keep the "AEGIS" name in its repo title;
  any binary distributions you build for personal / internal use
  can keep the original branding.

---

## What you need permission for

- **Using the AEGIS name in a product you sell.**
  Naming your own SaaS / commercial product "AEGIS Cloud," "AEGIS
  for Snowflake," or anything that implies official endorsement
  needs a written agreement.
- **Modified forks distributed to third parties under the AEGIS
  name.**
  If you change behaviour materially and ship a binary to other
  users still calling itself "AEGIS," that risks confusing
  downstream users about what is/isn't supported. Rename the
  fork (e.g., "AEGIS-foo," "ourCompany-aegis-fork") or seek a
  written compatibility statement.
- **Logo placement implying partnership.**
  Putting the AEGIS shield in your marketing site, sales deck,
  or pitch materials in a way that implies AEGIS endorses your
  product needs permission.
- **Merchandise.**
  Stickers, swag, conference handouts bearing the AEGIS logo for
  sale need permission. We'll probably say yes for non-commercial
  community swag — but ask.
- **Domain names containing "aegis" alongside generic agent /
  security terms** (e.g., `aegis-firewall.io`, `aegis-mcp.com`).
  Ask first to avoid downstream rename pain.

## Why this is a separate document from LICENSE

The MIT license, by design, only covers copyright in the source
code. It does **not** transfer ownership of the trademark to
downstream users. That's true of every MIT-licensed project
(React, Express, Tauri, etc. all have similar trademark policies).
The point isn't to be restrictive — it's to keep the name from
being claimed by a third party who could then prevent its use by
the project itself.

---

## How to ask

Email [aojieyua@usc.edu](mailto:aojieyua@usc.edu) with:

- A one-paragraph description of how you want to use the name/logo
- Where it will appear (URL, deck slide, etc.)
- Whether it's commercial or non-commercial

Reasonable requests will typically get a yes. The non-answer state
is no — please don't assume permission.

---

## Changes to this policy

Significant changes (broadening or narrowing what's permitted)
will be announced in the [CHANGELOG](CHANGELOG.md) and apply to
new uses only — already-permitted uses don't retroactively become
violations.
